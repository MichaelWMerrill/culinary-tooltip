Every cookout starts with the same anxious question: **how much meat do I buy?** Get it wrong and you either run out an hour in or eat leftovers for a week. The mistake most people make is planning around the *cooked* weight — when the number that matters at the butcher is the *raw* weight.

## Step 1: How Much Cooked Meat Per Guest

A reasonable main-course plate, with sides:

- **Brisket or turkey:** ~0.5 lb cooked per guest.
- **Pulled pork:** ~0.33 lb cooked per guest.

Scale it to your crowd — lighter for snackers, heavier if you want leftovers or you are feeding a hungry crew.

## Step 2: The Shrinkage Tax

Here is the part people forget. Low-and-slow cooking is brutal on weight. A packer brisket loses to **trimming** (fat cap and hard fat) and again to **evaporation and rendering** during the cook. All told, a brisket finishes near **50% yield** — half the weight you bought.

So the raw target is not the cooked target. It is:

> **raw weight = cooked needed ÷ yield fraction**

To serve **10 lb of cooked brisket**, you buy closer to **20 lb of raw packer**. Pulled pork lands around 50–60% yield; turkey around 65–75%. Bone-in, grade, and whether you brine all move the number.
